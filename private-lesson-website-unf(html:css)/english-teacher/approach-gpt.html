Approach

A teaching approach rooted in brutal honesty involves delivering clear, candid feedback to students, emphasizing both strengths and areas needing improvement without sugar-coating. This method fosters resilience and growth by confronting weaknesses head-on, encouraging self-awareness and accountability. It's crucial to balance honesty with empathy, ensuring critiques are constructive and aimed at fostering development. This approach prepares students for real-world challenges, cultivating a mindset geared toward continuous improvement and personal excellence.

"Lost in translation" refers to the meaning or nuances of a phrase, word, or concept being misinterpreted, misunderstood, or diminished when converted from one language to another. This often happens because languages have unique idioms, cultural references, and connotations that may not have direct equivalents in other languages. As a result, the original intent, humor, or subtlety can be altered or lost entirely. The expression highlights the challenges and limitations of translating text or speech accurately between different linguistic and cultural contexts.

Using American expressions and filler words like "like," "um," or "you know" as a non-American does not always make you sound more fluent or natural. These words are often used unconsciously by native speakers, and their use can be very nuanced. If used incorrectly or too frequently, they can make your speech sound forced or awkward instead of fluent. True fluency comes from understanding and using the language naturally, including its rhythm and context, rather than just adding fillers. It's better to focus on clear, confident communication rather than trying to mimic every casual speech pattern.
